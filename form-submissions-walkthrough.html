The question

Who can read a completed form?

Who's allowed to fill in a form is already decided. Who's allowed to read a form after it's been filled in is not.

Right now the system glues them together: if you can fill a form, you can read what others filled in — and if you can't, you can't. We think that's the wrong default.

Scope for today: only opening one specific completed form — a single EO coaching write-up, one Fellow's record about one school. Not the charts and dashboards; those follow the area each person covers and are handled automatically.

The shape of it

It's really two questions, stacked.

Before "who needs to read this," there's a question underneath it: are these two people even in groups that should share at all?

A completed form
Gate 1 · between groups

Can one group read another group's forms at all?

e.g. should government read what a partner Fellow wrote — or the reverse?

Gate 2 · within that

Who specifically needs to read it?

The person's manager? Whoever's responsible for that school?

→ readable
If Gate 1 says no, Gate 2 never gets asked. That's why we settle the groups first.

Decision 4 · what earns access

Why do you get to read someone else's form?

A Fellow records data about School X. The EO responsible for School X didn't fill it in, and isn't the Fellow's manager. Should they read it — just because it's their school?

So what makes a form one you can read?

Decision 5 · read vs fill

Read-only, or always able to fill too?

Are there forms people need to read but would never fill in themselves — like an EO reading a Fellow's school data?

Decision 6 · sensitive forms

Anything we keep deliberately narrow?

Coaching and performance write-ups are the obvious candidates — readable only by the author and maybe one reviewer, even for people who'd otherwise have a reason.

If we're short on time: this is the one to park for a quick follow-up — it won't block the engineers the way the others do.
